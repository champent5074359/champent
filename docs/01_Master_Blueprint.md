# Master Blueprint

## 1. Product Purpose

BusinessOS is a business operating system for small businesses and SMEs. It centralizes the daily back-office work of business owners and staff, from product and stock management to transactions, operating costs, suppliers, planning, and performance tracking.

## 2. Target Businesses

- Apparel and fashion stores
- Restaurants and food businesses
- Retail shops
- Other small businesses that sell products and operate one or more branches

## 3. Core Principles

- **Multi-business:** A user may work with one or more businesses.
- **Multi-branch:** Each business may have multiple branches.
- **Branch-aware data:** Operational records such as stock, sales, purchases, income, and expenses belong to a branch.
- **Single source of truth:** Products, transactions, and financial-operational records are connected in one platform.
- **Incremental delivery:** Build the smallest reliable foundation first, then expand workflows by sprint.

## 4. Core Domains

| Domain | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Organization | Business, branch, user, and future roles |
| Catalog | Products and categories |
| Operations | Inventory, suppliers, purchases, and events |
| Transactions | Sales, sale items, income, and expenses |
| Planning | Goals and future reporting |

## 5. Initial Scope

Sprint 1 establishes project structure and documentation only. No UI, database migration, authentication, or production code is implemented in this sprint.

## 6. Success Criteria

- The team has a consistent folder structure for future implementation.
- Product, data, flow, UX/UI, and development decisions are documented.
- The first database entities and relationships are clear enough to begin Sprint 2 schema design.
